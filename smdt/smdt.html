An Implementation of the Swedish Musical Discrimination Test

This site contains an implementation of the Swedish Musical Discrimination Test.

It's a test that was intended to assess the user's ability to differentiate between audio samples, but was also used as a proxy for music ability in a followup study.

You can find more information about the test here.

The test was made up of three different subtests:

  1. A melody test, which tests whether the participant can identify the specific note changed between two melodies
  2. A rhythm test, which tests whether the participant can tell if two rhythms are different
  3. A pitch test, which tests whether the user can differentiate between two similar pitches

Melody Test

In this test we play one melody, and play the melody again with 1 note changed. This test involves identifying the note changed by clicking the note that changed. (Alternatively, press 1-9 on the keyboard corresponding to the note.)

(And yes, this is effectively a memory test.)

Rhythm Test

In this test, we play a short rhythm, and then play the rhythm again possibly with a modification to it. This test involves identifying whether we played an identical rhythm or a different one. Click the corresponding button (alternatively, press 'i' for identical/'d' for different).

Pitch Test

In this test, we play two tones one after the other. The second tone will always be different, but may be higher or lower than the first tone. This test involves identifying whether the second tone is higher or lower. Click the corresponding button (alternatively, press 'h' for higher and 'l' for lower).
